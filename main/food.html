FOOD

Food From All Over China

Hot Pot

Orgin: Northern China

Diners cook raw ingredients like thinly sliced meats, seafood, vegetables, tofu, and noodles in a simmering pot of flavorful broth at the table, dipping them to cook and then enjoying them with various sauces.

Peking Duck

Orgin: Beijing and Nanjing

Peking duck is a famous Chinese dish known for its crispy skin and succulent meat, traditionally served with thin pancakes, scallions, cucumber, and a sweet bean or plum sauce.

Bingtanghulu

Orign: Northern China

Bingtang hulu is a traditional Chinese snack consisting of several malt sugar coated fruits of Chinese hawthorn on a bamboo skewer. It is typically made by skewering hawthorn fruits and coating them in heated sugar syrup, which hardens in the cold.

ZongZi

Orign: China

Zongzi is a traditional Chinese dish of glutinous rice dumplings filled with various ingredients, wrapped in bamboo leaves, and steamed or boiled. They can be salty (with pork, mushrooms, salted egg yolk) or sweet (with red bean paste, jujubes) and are a popular food for the Dragon Boat Festival.
