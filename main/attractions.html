Attractions List

The Great Wall of China

Location: Northern China

The Great Wall is one of the most famous structures in the world. It was built to protect ancient China from invasions and stretches over 13,000 miles. It was built under the first emperor Qin Shi Huang around 221 BCE.

The Forbidden City

Location: Beijing

The Forbidden City is a massive palace complex that served as the home to 24 Chinese emperors. It has the world's largest ancient wooden structure, featuring symmetrical halls, courtyards, gardens, and decorative colors like red (fortune) and yellow (imperial power). The palace holds over 1.8 million artifacts, from imperial robes to ceramics, reflecting 5,000 years of Chinese history.

The Terracotta Army

Location: Xi'an

The Terracotta Army consists of thousands of life-sized soldiers buried to protect China's first emperor in the afterlife.

Harbin Ice & Snow World

Location: Harbin

Harbin Ice and Snow World is a massive, magical winter theme park in China, part of the Harbin Ice Festival. It features gigantic, illuminated castles, palaces, and sculptures carved entirely from ice and snow. It is a glowing city with ice slides, ice skating, performances, restaurants, and many winter activities.

Zhang Jia Jie

Location: Hunan

Zhangjiajie, in China's Hunan Province, is famous for its fantasy like landscapes, particularly the quartz sandstone pillars, like the "Avatar" mountains. It has misty peaks, deep canyons ,with the world's highest glass bridge, lush forests, and nature-lover's paradise.
